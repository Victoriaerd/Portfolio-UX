Vegan AI app

A concept mobile app exploring how AI-powered nutritional guidance could support plant-based living.

Roles

UX/UI, Research, Branding, App Design (Personal Project)


Challenge

Create an engaging, user-friendly mobile experience that makes veganism approachable and sustainable long-term. After several years as a vegan, I recognised a gap in existing solutions: people needed more than recipes, they needed nutritional education and confidence.

Design Goals

Clean, modern interface with bright, food-inspired colours. Accessible to users with varying technical abilities. Evidence-based nutritional guidance without overwhelming new vegans.

Tools

Figma, Premiere and Photoshop.


Research & Discovery

User Interviews

I conducted interviews with 8 participants - 4 existing vegans and 4 considering the switch. The key finding shifted my entire approach: people weren't struggling with finding recipes (Instagram provided endless inspiration). Instead, they lacked confidence in nutritional adequacy. As one participant noted, "I almost had to become a nutritionist."


Key insight: Users needed a nutritional coach that could also cook, not another recipe app.

Personas

As part of my design process, I developed 2 personas to help me better understand and empathise with the target audience for the app. Valeria - a long-term vegan focused on optimal nutrition and athletic performance, and Patrick - an aspiring vegan concerned about health impacts and ease of transition.

Competitive Analysis

I analysed existing vegan and meal planning apps: Yummly, Happy Cow, Forks Over Knives to identify gaps. Most focused on recipes or restaurant finding. None provided comprehensive nutritional education paired with meal planning.


Problem Statement

"Many people struggle to transition to a vegan lifestyle due to lack of knowledge, resources, and support. How might we create a comprehensive app that provides guidance, education, and motivation to help users successfully adopt and maintain a vegan lifestyle?"

Design Process

Features prioritisation

Using an impact/effort matrix, I prioritised features based on user needs. High-priority features included an AI nutritional assistant capitalising on ChatGPT's emergence, customisable meal planner, integrated nutritional tracker, and recipe search with dietary filters.

Information Architecture

I mapped user flows for core journeys: onboarding, meal planning, and cooking. The structure prioritised quick access to the AI assistant and meal plans whilst keeping nutritional tracking seamlessly integrated.


User Flow


Wireframes

Initial wireframes established the overall structure and hierarchy, focusing on intuitive navigation and prominent display of key features.


Prototype

Personalised Onboarding


The sign-up flow gathers fitness goals, dietary restrictions, cooking ability, and preferences to create tailored meal plans. This ensures both experienced vegans and beginners receive appropriate recommendations.


Meal Planner


Users can browse recipes with robust filtering:cuisine, nutrition goals, cooking time, ingredients on hand or let the app generate weekly plans. The pantry feature allows voice input of existing ingredients to minimise waste. Plans sync directly to personal calendars.

The app's filtering system helps Patrick to select a meal that perfectly suits his special occasion, ensuring a memorable dining experience. And Valeria can effortlessly identify the most nutritious options that align with her dietary preferences and goals.

The app enables users to make the most of their pantry ingredients, minimising food waste.

AI Assistant


The feature emerged mid-project with ChatGPT's release. The AI assistant provides recipe suggestions based on available ingredients, nutritional guidance, vegan substitutes for favourite recipes, cooking tips tailored to experience level, and lifestyle advice on ethics and nutrition.

Grocery Shopping


Seamless integration allows users to add ingredients from meal plans directly to shopping lists, with flexible quantity adjustments and easy addition of extra items.

Cooking Mode


Step-by-step instructions with hands-free navigation, integrated timers, and automatic nutritional sync to the tracker. Adjustable serving sizes and the ability to save favourites.


Nutritional Tracker


Automatic tracking of macro and micronutrients with visual breakdowns. Barcode scanner for additional foods. Syncs with wearables for comprehensive health data.

Impact Calculator


Motivational feature tracking environmental impact: water saved, carbon footprint reduction, grain conservation, and animal lives saved. Gamified user levels encourage continued engagement.


Testing & Iteration

I conducted usability tests with 4 participants matching my personas 2 vegans, 2 considering veganism. Testing occurred in two rounds with design iterations between.

Key findings and changes:

  1. Moved barcode scanner, voice input, and search from top of screen to thumb-friendly lower position based on feedback about uncomfortable reach
  2. Added back buttons to sign-up flow for revising choices
  3. Clarified pantry instructions
  4. Added intermittent fasting options and separated recipes by meal type (breakfast, lunch, dinner)

Accessibility

Colour contrast tested with Colour Contrast Analyser. Used off-white backgrounds with grey-blue text to reduce eye strain.

All interactive elements meet WCAG 2.1 minimum 44x44px touch targets. Tested with colour blindness simulation plugin.


Reflection

This speculative project allowed me to explore AI integration in behaviour change apps and practice end-to-end mobile UX design. Users responded positively to the comprehensive approach, particularly the AI assistant and seamless grocery integration. They appreciated the vibrant colour choices and found the features met their expectations.


What I'd do differently: With only 4 test participants, validation was limited. A real product would require more extensive testing, particularly around the AI assistant's accuracy and nutritional tracking reliability. I'd also explore which features genuinely drive retention versus which are "nice-to-have," and investigate user requests for workout routines.


Key learning: User research fundamentally shifted my approach from "recipe app" to "nutritional coach." This reinforced the importance of interviewing users before designing solutions.

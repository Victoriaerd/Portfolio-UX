Vegan AI app

Your plant-based lifestyle assistant.


Vegan AI is a revolutionary mobile app service designed to simplify and support living a vegan lifestyle. It provides an intuitive, welcoming platform to educate, inspire, and empower vegans at any stage of their journey. We're on a mission to make plant-based living easy, enjoyable, and accessible to all. Let Vegan AI help you thrive on a vegan diet.

Role

  1. UX Reserch
  2. UI
  3. Branding
  4. Application Design

Challenge

To create a prototype for an engaging, user-friendly mobile experience for aspiring plant-based eaters that makes veganism approachable, enjoyable, and sustainable long-term.

The lifestyle shift to veganism can be daunting for many people, leading to frustrations. Having switched to the vegan lifestyle over 7 years ago I found there is a need for a supportive platform that provides educational resources, simplifies meal planning/shopping, and offers inspiration to help new vegans thrive.

My initial idea was to create a cooking app full of vegan recipes that would allow people to get inspiration along with the ability to veganise their favourite recipes as they switched to a new lifestyle.

The goal is creating an app that simplifies veganism and sets users up for sustainable plant-based success.

Design Goals:

The app should have a clean, modern design with an intuitive user interface. The colour palette should be bright and colorful, reflecting the natural vibrancy of plant-based foods. The design should be easy to use and accessible to people with a range of technical abilities.

Tools:

I used Figma, Premiere and Photoshop for this project.


The process

I applied Design thinking as a problem-solving approach that emphasises empathy, creativity, and iterative prototyping. It consists of 5 phases: Empathise, Define, Ideate, Prototye & Test, and Refine & Improve.

Research

While I was familiar with the subject before conducting user interviews, I wanted to gain a deeper understanding of the topic and the existing products on the market. Research has shown the significant impact of agriculture on climate change, which motivated me to delve deeper into the subject.
Amanda Schupak who writes for The Guardian emphasises the importance of transitioning to sustainable, low-emissions diets, even if not fully vegan:

"What we eat has an enormous environmental impact. Scientists estimate that food production causes 35% of planet-warming greenhouse gas emissions, with meat responsible for more than twice the pollution of fruits, grains and greens. In April, the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) report urged world leaders, especially those in developed countries, to support a transition to sustainable, healthy, low-emissions diets to help mitigate the worst effects of the climate crisis."

A study by Marco Springmann suggests that transitioning to plant-based diets could reduce global mortality and food-related greenhouse gas emissions:

"Transitioning toward more plant-based diets that are in line with standard dietary guidelines could reduce global mortality by 6-10% and food-related greenhouse gas emissions by 29-70% compared with a reference scenario in 2050."

The above quotes emphasise the urgency of making changes to our food system to address the interconnected issues of climate change, health, and ecosystem degradation. The research highlighted the significant impact of food production and consumption on the environment and human health.

User Interviews

Given that the subject of veganism can be sensitive, I felt that interviews would allow me to ask follow-up questions and gain a more in-depth understanding of the audience's motivations and needs. I conducted semi-structured interviews both with vegans and people who are just curious about the subject. I selected a set of pre-determined topics that I knew would be relevant to my project, but also allowed for flexibility to explore new avenues and follow-up questions as they arose adapting to each person. The interviews provided me with a more nuanced understanding of the challenges and opportunities in designing a successful vegan app. I used the following questions as a basis of the interviews.


  1. Motivations/ inspiration for considering vegan diet? If vegan motivation for staying vegan and inspiration when they first switched.
  2. Struggles and challenges they encountered/envision to encounter when first switched?
  3. Do they currently keep track of nutrition and food intake?
  4. How do they discover new recipes?
  5. What are your biggest concerns when it comes to vegan nutrition?
  6. What apps do they use to that are related to the diet?
  7. What is missing from their routines that an app could help with?
  8. Any inspiration that you encountered while switching to the vegan diet / wish there was more of?

Personas

As part of my design process, I developed 2 personas to help me better understand and empathise with the target audience for the app. I wanted to target both people already familiar with the vegan lifestyle and users who are just considering to switch. I created detailed profiles and during user interviews I was able to gain valuable insights into their expectations, concerns, motivations, and behaviours. Valeria and Patrick served as a guide for the rest of the design process, helping me to keep the user's needs and goals at the forefront of my thinking.

Key Findings

During the interviews it became clear that people really struggled with the luck of nutritional education when it came to the vegan diet. As one participant pointed out, "I almost had to become a nutritionist." People who were not yet vegan were concerned that their health would be impacted due to the lack of knowledge, with one saying, "it is too difficult, and I would struggle to eat well."


While people had some knowledge of the vegan lifestyle and its environmental impact, it seemed that they didn't just want another recipe app, as people who were vegan were following influencers on Instagram and getting inspiration there, and omnivores had a few tried and tested recipes they cooked. Instead, what people needed was a nutritional coach that also knew how to cook well. In response to these findings, the initial product idea was adjusted to better accommodate the need for nutritional education and support.

Competitive Analysis

I identified companies and products that are currently on the market and are aiming to help the transitioning to a vegan diet. I wanted to gain insights into existing solutions that aim to make the transition process more seamless and convenient.

To assess the strengths and weaknesses of my competitors I compared their content, functionality, and overall user experience. I also looked into user reviews on the app store. This exercise enabled me to pinpoint areas of improvement and identify potential gaps in the market that my app could address effectively.

Problem Statement

A well-crafted problem statement will help me guide the design process by providing a clear and focused objective to work towards, while also addressing the needs and pain points of the target audience:

"Many people tend to struggle to transition to a vegan lifestyle due to a lack of knowledge, resources, and support. My goal is to create a comprehensive and user-friendly app that provides guidance, education, and motivation to help users successfully adopt and most importantly maintain a vegan lifestyle in the long run."

Ideate

Affinity Map

I used affinity map as a visual tool for brainstorming and organising information. It helped me to make sense of the data gathered in research, insights from the interviews, and ideas by grouping related concepts or themes together to come up with solutions and features for my target audience. The process involved capturing pain points or insights from the user interviews and then organising them based on their natural relationships or a common theme.

Features prioritisation

After conducting research, interviews and analysis, I developed a comprehensive list of solutions and features tailored to address the pain points identified among the target audience. Throughout this process, I referenced the personas I had previously created to ensure alignment with their needs and preferences.

To prioritise the features, I utilised a chart that considered the potential impact and level of effort required for implementation. Emphasising high impact and low effort, as well as high impact and high effort, took precedence over features with low impact and high effort.

During the course of this case study, an exciting development emerged with the introduction of ChatGPT, an advanced AI assistant. This breakthrough created an opportunity to enhance the app by incorporating a vegan AI assistant capable of answering user queries regarding nutrition, supplementation, recipe suggestions, and providing guidance throughout their transition to a vegan diet. An integration with ChatGPT or a similar LLM is a resource that would allow to address user's inquiries comprehensively.

Other main features I decided to focus on:

  1. Meal Planner - a fully customisable meal plan feature to allow users to create their own meal plans from scratch.
  2. Integrated nutritional tracker.
  3. Integration with a grocery delivery service to make it easier for users to purchase the ingredients.
  4. A recipe search and recipe categorisation.
  5. Prompts to drink more water.
  6. Adding ingredients user already has to help reduce waste.
  7. Progress tracking to stay motivated.
  8. Hands-free cooking mode.
  9. Impact calculator to help staying on track.

Information Architecture

I next worked on site map and user flow.

IA serves as the backbone of the app, ensuring that users can effortlessly access and interact with the content, contributing to overall user satisfaction and the app's effectiveness in meeting user needs.

Efficiantly organised information enables efficient task completion, reduces cognitive load, and fosters a sense of trust in the app.

After organising information I was able to start wortkign on low fidelity wireframes.


User Flow

In order to identify pain points and opportunities I mapped the user flow. I kept personas I created in mind durign this process and reffered back to the data I discovered durign interviews

I incorporated a user flow into the information architechture map to get a better understanding of the user journey, the user flows also played a crucial role in informing the wireframe process. It aided in the creation of a structure and hierarchy that aligns with the user experience.


Wireframes

To establish the overall structure and layout of the app, I sketched out wireframes. I was focusing on defining the placement and arrangement of key components, such as navigation bars, content sections, and buttons.

I wanted to determine the hierarchy of content within each screen so that the most important information is prominently displayed. I focused on representing the core functionality of the app.


Prototype

Sign in Process


Based on my persona's needs, pain points, and motivations, I proceeded to develop high-fidelity wireframes. The initial focus was on the sign-up process, aiming to create a user-friendly and comprehensive experience. This would allow users to create personalised plans based on their fitness goals, dietary restrictions, food preferences, dislikes, as well as important factors like gender, age, and activity levels. Different levels of cooking were taken into account to accomodate different abilities for Patrick and Valeria. The goal was to gather detailed information to ensure the app could provide tailored recommendations and support for each user persona's needs.


Meal Planner


I then created a comprehensive meal planning feature for the app. This included a diverse range of recipes, a convenient pantry section that allowes users to input ingredients through voice commands, and intuitive search filters to cater to individual dietary needs. Additionally, I added a scheduling functionality, enabling users to easily integrate the meal plans into their personal calendars. These features were designed to enhance user convenience and provide a seamless experience in meal planning and organisation.

The app's robust filtering system empowers Patrick to select a meal that perfectly suits his special occasion, ensuring a memorable dining experience. On the other hand, Valeria can effortlessly identify the most nutritious options that align with her dietary preferences and goals. The app enables users to make the most of their pantry ingredients, minimising food waste and promoting sustainability. Taking it a step further, the integration of an AI assistant will provide invaluable support by addressing any vegan lifestyle-related queries, offering personalised guidance and enhancing the overall user experience.


AI Assistant


AI assistant functionality is based on the recent developments in language models such as chatGPT. It will assist in a variety of ways. Users can ask the AI assistant for vegan recipe ideas based on specific ingredients, dietary preferences, or cuisines. It will generate recipe recommendations and provide detailed instructions. Users will be able to inquire about the nutritional content of various foods, such as protein, vitamins, or minerals which will be helpful to both Valeria and Patrick.

Patricks can also ask the AI assistant for vegan substitutes for his favourite non-vegan ingredients in his favourite recipes to help with his transition. The AI can also offer guidance on how to achieve similar flavours and textures. It can offer cooking tips and techniques specifically tailored to vegan cooking, as well as user's cooking expertise level making it easier for someone who is just switching to vegan lifestyle. Users are able to get advice and guidance on various aspects of the lifestyle: ethical considerations, environmental impact, plant-based nutrition all within the app.

Grocery Shopping


I wanted to integrate groccery shopping within the app to allows users to seamlessly add ingredients from their meal plans to their shopping list. This integration ensures a smooth transition from planning meals to purchasing the necessary items. It also helps users keep track of their pantry and refrigerator items.

Based on initial user interviews, I have designed a flexible grocery shopping list feature where the quantities of items can be easily adjusted according to their needs. Additional items can also be easily added in an online store.


Cooking


When designing cooking mode I included ingredients, measurements, and cooking times as well as timer when needed for different steps. I also included hands-free cooking feature in step by step mode. Users can adjust the recipe quantities based on the number of servings they need. Users are able to provide ratings and save favourite recipes.

Nutritional Information is displayed for each recipe and is synced automatically with the food tracker to help monitor nutritional intake.


Food Tracker


I wanted to enable users to track their nutrients intake, including macronutrients (such as carbohydrates, proteins, and fats) and micronutrients such as vitamins and minerals. The nutritional breakdown is provided for each meal as well as the summary of the user's overall nutrient intake. I decided to integrate a barcode scanner functionality that allows users to add any additional foods. This feature can automatically retrieve nutritional information from a database. Users will also be able to customise and create recipes from scratch. Provided visual representations offer insights based on their tracked data to help users make informed decisions about their dietary choices this feature in works in conjunction with AI assistant.

Based on initial user interviews I also included a feature to track daily water intake, encouraging users to stay hydrated.

The food tracker can be synced with wearable devices or other health apps to gather additional data, such as calorie expenditure or physical activity, for a more comprehensive overview of the user's health and wellness. Users can amend preferences or allergens in their profile.

Impact Calculator


To encourage users to stay on track I wanted to provide environmental impact information. Such as litres of water saved, carbon footprint calculation, deforestation impact analysis, amount of grains saved and of course animal lives saved.

A colourful ring graphic represents the data in a visually engaging way to inspire users as they interact with the information.

To enhance motivation and enable progress tracking, I introduced user levels. These levels provide a sense of achievement and allow users to monitor their progress as they engage with the app's features and accomplish various milestones.

User Testing

After completing the prototype, I proceeded with user testing, choosing four individuals from the previous participant list who closely matched the characteristics of my personas. I conducted interviews and observations with two individuals, implemented the necessary changes based on their feedback, and then interviewed the remaining two participants using the updated prototypes.

During the testing session, I posed the following questions:

  1. What are your initial impressions of the app's design and user interface? I observed if it was easy to navigate and asked if anything stands out positively or negatively.
  2. Can you describe your experience using the app? Were you able to understand and use the app's features easily?
  3. Were there any aspects of the app that you found confusing or difficult to use? I also asked about specific functionality and if it was useful such as meal planning and using AI assistant and observed users perform a task.
  4. Does the app address your needs and expectations as someone who is vegan or is considering vegan diet? Are there any key features or functionalities missing that you would like to see in the app?
  5. Do you find interface and design visually appealing? Are the icons, buttons, and other interface elementsclear and understandable?
  6. Were you able to accomplish your intended tasks or goals using the app? How would you rate your overall satisfaction with the app and its functionality?
  7. Are there any specific pain points or areas that you think could be enhanced?
  8. Do you have any general feedback or suggestions for improving the app?

Iterations

I made the followign changes to the initial design based on conducted user tests:

  1. Based on feedback, I made adjustments to the initial design placement of the barcode scanner, voice input, and search bar. Initially, they were positioned at the top of the screen, but I moved them down to better accommodate user preferences and improve usability: "it's uncomforbotable having to reach for the top of the screen".
  2. I incorporated back buttons into my designs, particularly in the sign-up process, as I noticed that users lacked the ability to revise their choices.
  3. I also added intermittent fasting as a dieting option and separated recepies by breakfast, lunch and dinner categories based on user feedback.
  4. The pantry step of the journey needed some additional instructions initially.
  5. To address some confusion, I took the feedback into account and renamed the buttons in the "create a meal plan" journey. This helped provide clearer indications of each step's purpose, making it easier for users to navigate through the prototype.

Accessibility

I used Colour Contrast Analyser app to ensure that there is an adequate contrast between elements within the website. This helps users with low vision or those in different lighting conditions to effectively use the app. I tested not only the text but also various UI elements to make sure it was legible.

I used off-white colour as a background and not completely black but black with a touch of grey blue for text. Research suggests that less contrast in the case of black text on white background as well as white text on black background works better for accessibility as it reduces strain on the eyes

To insure icons in the menu are large enough and easy to click I used frames that are at least 44x44px according to WCAG 2.1.

To make sure the pages were legible for people who are colour blind I used a Figma plugin.


Summary and what's next

Overall users found that as it met their expectations in terms of the features and they appreciated the vibrant colour choices. Users particularly liked the seamless integration of the grocery shopping and were excited about the possibilities of havign a vegan AI assistant. However, there was a common desire among users for the inclusion of workout routines within the app. This feedback provides an opportunity for future iterations to consider incorporating this feature.

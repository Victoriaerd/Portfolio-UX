I'm a UX designer based in London, specialising in research-driven design for e-commerce and complex digital tools.

Over the past decade, I've worked with organisations ranging from Heal's and the National Theatre to Diageo and the British Council, creating visual content and digital experiences that connect with audiences. For the past few years, I've focused specifically on UX research and interaction design.

My approach centres on understanding people first. I conduct user research - interviews, usability testing, analytics analysis - to uncover the problems worth solving. Then I design solutions that balance user needs with business objectives and technical feasibility.

Recent projects include:

  • Redesigning Heal's product pages to improve discoverability and conversion

  • Creating an internal transcription tool for FreeUK Genealogy volunteers

  • Developing AI-assisted mobile experiences for behaviour change

I'm particularly drawn to projects where accessibility matters, and where design decisions need to be evidence-based rather than assumption-driven.

An unconventional path

Before UX, I spent years as a photographer and videographer, leading visual content strategy at Heal's and documenting performances at the National Theatre. Before that, I worked in corporate finance working on cross-border transactions.

This diverse background gives me a useful perspective: I understand business metrics and ROI (the economics degree), visual storytelling and brand consistency (the photography years), and how to translate complex requirements into intuitive experiences (the UX work).

Skills and tools

Research: User interviews, usability testing, journey mapping, analytics (Google Analytics), heuristic evaluation

Design: Figma, Miro, prototyping, design systems, accessibility

Development: HTML/CSS for prototyping and collaboration with engineers

Visual: Photography, videography, Adobe Creative Suite, 3D/CGI project management

Currently

I'm open to mid-level UX positions where I can contribute to thoughtful, research-driven design and continue growing alongside talented teams.
